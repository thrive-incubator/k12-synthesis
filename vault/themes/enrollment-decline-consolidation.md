## Current snapshot

The K-12 revenue base is shrinking structurally, not cyclically, and district leaders now say so
themselves: budget shortfalls and declining enrollment top their concerns in a new survey. An
earlier analysis projects enrollment declines could cost states **$11.5 billion annually by
2030-31** in lost per-pupil funding. The strain is visible in this week's budgets — Chicago's
$9.88B 2026-27 budget landed amid deepening strain and a furlough fight, and a record number of
Indiana districts are asking voters for property-tax increases — and in real estate: closures are
becoming charter capacity (a French immersion charter is moving into a shuttered Denver district
building, after Alaska's 12 closures and Detroit's low-enrollment list). This is the framework
within which all downstream district purchasing — including wellbeing spending — now sits.
*(as of 2026-W30)*
