## Current snapshot

The K-12 revenue base is shrinking structurally, not cyclically, and districts are consolidating in
response. A new analysis projects enrollment declines could cost states **$11.5 billion annually by
2030-31** in lost per-pupil funding. The operational face of that is school closures: Alaska
districts closed **12 schools** this year amid severe budget cuts, and Detroit's DPSCD added five
more buildings to its low-enrollment, high-cost list. This is the framework within which all
downstream district purchasing — including wellbeing spending — now sits.
*(as of 2026-W27)*
