## Current snapshot

The restructuring is advancing and being contested at once, with special education as the front
line. The ED–HHS interagency agreement would move IDEA formula and discretionary grants,
compliance and monitoring, and state performance determinations to HHS — one of **14 agreements
ED has signed with six agencies** in the past year — and ED's proposed FY27 budget would cut
special-education (OSERS) staff from 163 to 31. Opposition is broadening: special-ed
professionals and former officials at the Legislative Summit, and 86 House Democrats demanding
reversal — while House Republicans advanced a 10-bill package to make transfers permanent. IDEA
currently funds $15.5B in FY26 services for roughly 8.2 million students, with growing student
mental-health demand named among the top unmet needs. Net effect unchanged: states and districts
inherit growing ambiguity about where federal K12 money and rules will come from.
*(as of 2026-W30)*
