## Current snapshot

The **$1B in Bipartisan Safer Communities Act school mental-health grants is at a decision
point**: despite the December 19, 2025 injunction, ED is moving to permanently end the grants
under a new legal theory — "termination" under 2 C.F.R. § 200.340, after courts blocked
"discontinuation" under 34 C.F.R. § 75.253 — with a hearing set for **July 24, 2026** and, per
ED's own motion, terminations possible as early as **July 31**. The Washington-led 15-state suit
and a protective Connecticut filing are trying to stop it, and Judge Talwani separately rejected
the administration's "agency priorities" reading of that same OMB clause in a 21-AG case covering
$5.4B in grants. The stakes are concrete: nationally the grants have funded 1,300 mental-health
professionals serving 775,000 students; in Texas, two at-risk BSCA-funded programs carry 86% of
school mental-health funding across 2,500+ campuses, and the legislature declined a dedicated
state allotment — federal instability is not being backfilled. SAMHSA's integration and
behavioral-health-PHI NOFOs close July 27; the CCBHC trio closes August 17.
*(as of 2026-W30)*

## Timeline

### 2026-W30 — ED moves to terminate the $1B grants under a new legal theory; hearing July 24
*The 74 · July 17, 2026* · [link](https://www.the74million.org/article/connecticut-sues-again-over-withheld-school-mental-health-grants/)
Despite the December 19, 2025 injunction, ED now cites 2 C.F.R. § 200.340 ("termination") instead
of the blocked 34 C.F.R. § 75.253 ("discontinuation") pathway; a hearing on the distinction is set
for July 24, and per ED's own motion terminations could begin as early as July 31. Connecticut AG
William Tong filed again "protectively," alongside the 15-state suit led by Washington
(also: [Islands' Sounder, July 19](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMi3gFBVV95cUxNUEIyTnhFY0dWa09DbVYteVZkajNTN1ZNUi1QbDJFVVdPelVHanQwTHJZTTNkYlhjbFNSQnplN1J1bDlKT3VKNldLZWdOTU1kNVh5SWg1dG5CZWNyRTBUQ0kxaC1qWWJEWC0zQTQtcVFqbGd6NmVDd2k1eEhUcDczZ2xYamNwRW1Ud2tyeUxST1dpenVoNW5pcU00V3lOR1YtS1VtaG9ubmJ4ZWtCUVE2N3JLOXFNOVFXVXoxcDBoNUR6RllMS2xteDBKX08wcENSam9mWVZ2YUdKMUZHZUE?oc=5)).
Per the CT AG's office, the grants have funded 1,300 mental-health professionals serving 775,000
K-12 students, with suicide rates halved in participating high-need schools.

### 2026-W30 — Federal judge rejects "agency priorities" as grounds for grant revocation
*K-12 Dive · July 20, 2026* · [link](https://www.k12dive.com/news/trump-officials-cant-revoke-grants-due-to-new-agency-goals-judge-rules/825719/)
Siding with 21 attorneys general and three governors holding $5.4B in existing grants, U.S.
District Judge Indira Talwani ruled the administration's reading of the OMB clause — termination
when an award "no longer effectuates the program goals or agency priorities" — "is not clearly
supported by the text." The ruling blocks future priority-shift terminations; it does not undo
past cuts.

### 2026-W30 — Texas: two at-risk BSCA programs carry 86% of school mental-health funding
*The 74 · July 21, 2026* · [link](https://www.the74million.org/article/mental-health-programs-could-bear-the-brunt-of-600m-federal-cuts-to-texas-schools/)
At least $600M — 16.1% of Texas's federal K-12 funding, per a Learning Policy Institute analysis —
is being withheld; the Texas Center for Student Supports (60 high-need districts) and the Stronger
Connections Grant Program (98 districts) account for 86% of school mental-health funding across
2,500+ campuses, per Mental Health America of Greater Houston. The state's new $8.5B school
funding package is mostly earmarked for teacher pay; a dedicated student mental-health allotment
(HB 1257) failed.
