## Current snapshot

Chronic absenteeism is the wellbeing-adjacent problem policymakers are most willing to act on
right now, and attendance metrics are becoming the accountability frame through which wellbeing
investments get argued. States are adopting new attendance-recovery measures; on the ground, a
Memphis charter school cut chronic absenteeism to just three students, and Detroit's school-based
health hubs are being examined as an absenteeism intervention — school-based health infrastructure
justified in attendance terms. Attention is extending to the earliest grades: Hechinger examined
why kindergarten chronic absenteeism remains persistently high.
*(as of 2026-W30)*
