## Current snapshot

The classroom-AI land-grab is colliding with newly forming procurement gates, while adoption
keeps climbing — Instructure's new survey finds more students and educators embracing AI.
Anthropic launched Claude for Teachers, joining OpenAI and Google in the race for classrooms, the
same week NYC — the nation's largest district — held a pause on software purchases until its AI
guidance is final, and Illinois issued state guidance on classroom AI use. State and district AI
guidance documents are becoming the de facto procurement rulebook that every edtech sale
(wellbeing tools included) must pass through. The evidence layer is forming in parallel: a new
study maps the national K-12 AI policy landscape, and Hechinger reports that AI saves teachers
time but may cost students learning.
*(as of 2026-W30)*
