## Current snapshot

The U.S. Department of Education is defending a plan to move some special education functions to
other agencies, pushing back on "misconceptions" about how IDEA services would be administered under
the change. Separately, a new federal school choice program tied to the department is reported to
need heavy marketing spend to reach eligible families. Federal education funding reliability is under
strain this week — roughly 6,000 Maine students could lose school-based mental health services
because related federal funds are frozen in litigation.
