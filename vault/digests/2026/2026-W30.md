# Thrive K12 Funding & Wellbeing Synthesis
### Week of July 15 – July 22, 2026

## This Week's Throughline

The week's center of gravity is the $1 billion in Bipartisan Safer Communities Act school mental health grants, now at a genuine decision point: the Education Department is trying to end the grants under a new legal theory ("termination" under 2 C.F.R. § 200.340, after courts blocked "discontinuation"), a hearing is set for **July 24**, and by ED's own motion terminations could begin **July 31**. Fifteen states led by Washington plus a protective Connecticut suit are trying to stop it — while a separate ruling by Judge Talwani just rejected the administration's core theory that grants can be revoked over shifting "agency priorities," the very clause ED is invoking. Texas shows what's at stake downstream: two at-risk BSCA-funded programs carry 86% of school mental health funding on 2,500+ campuses, and the legislature declined to create a dedicated state mental health allotment — federal instability is not being backfilled. Beneath all of it, district budgets keep tightening (Chicago's $9.88B budget fight, record Indiana tax referenda, budget shortfalls topping district leaders' concerns), so wellbeing staffing increasingly competes in zero-sum local budgets. Two SAMHSA windows close **July 27**; the CCBHC trio closes August 17. As usual, district-layer signals here are inferred from budget journalism, not observed procurement.

## Federal Funding & Policy

### States sue again as ED moves to terminate the $1B school mental health grants — hearing July 24, terminations possible July 31
*The 74 · July 17, 2026* · [link](https://www.the74million.org/article/connecticut-sues-again-over-withheld-school-mental-health-grants/)

Despite a December 19, 2025 injunction, the Education Department is moving to permanently end the $1 billion in school-based mental health grants funded by the 2022 Bipartisan Safer Communities Act — now citing 2 C.F.R. § 200.340 ("termination") instead of the 34 C.F.R. § 75.253 ("discontinuation") pathway courts already blocked. A 15-state coalition led by Washington filed suit to stop it ([link](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMi3gFBVV95cUxNUEIyTnhFY0dWa09DbVYteVZkajNTN1ZNUi1QbDJFVVdPelVHanQwTHJZTTNkYlhjbFNSQnplN1J1bDlKT3VKNldLZWdOTU1kNVh5SWg1dG5CZWNyRTBUQ0kxaC1qWWJEWC0zQTQtcVFqbGd6NmVDd2k1eEhUcDczZ2xYamNwRW1Ud2tyeUxST1dpenVoNW5pcU00V3lOR1YtS1VtaG9ubmJ4ZWtCUVE2N3JLOXFNOVFXVXoxcDBoNUR6RllMS2xteDBKX08wcENSam9mWVZ2YUdKMUZHZUE?oc=5)), and Connecticut AG William Tong filed again "protectively"; a hearing on whether the injunction covers "termination" is set for July 24, and per ED's own motion, terminations could begin as early as July 31. The program's record to date, per the CT AG's office: 1,300 mental health professionals hired, 775,000 K-12 students served, and suicide rates halved in participating high-need schools.

**So what:** This is the single biggest live threat to federal school mental health money. July 24 and July 31 are the dates to watch.

### Federal judge rules grants can't be revoked over new "agency priorities"
*K-12 Dive · July 20, 2026* · [link](https://www.k12dive.com/news/trump-officials-cant-revoke-grants-due-to-new-agency-goals-judge-rules/825719/)

Siding with 21 attorneys general and three governors (led by New Jersey, Massachusetts, and New York) holding $5.4 billion in existing grants, U.S. District Judge Indira Talwani ruled that the administration's reading of the OMB grant regulation — that an award may be terminated if it "no longer effectuates the program goals or agency priorities" — "is not clearly supported by the text" and "runs counter to the regulatory scheme." The ruling blocks future terminations based on shifting administration priorities; it does not undo past cuts.

**So what:** The clause at issue is the same 2 C.F.R. § 200.340 language ED is now invoking against the school mental health grants — the two cases are converging on whether priority-shift terminations are legal at all.

### Special educators oppose moving IDEA administration from ED to HHS
*K-12 Dive · July 21, 2026* · [link](https://www.k12dive.com/news/special-educators-oppose-education-department-breakup-interagency-agreement-Health-Human-Services/825821/)

At the Special Education Legislative Summit, special education professionals and former ED officials opposed the interagency agreement moving IDEA formula and discretionary grants, compliance and monitoring, and state performance determinations to HHS — one of 14 agreements ED has signed with six agencies in the past year. ED's proposed FY27 budget would cut special education (OSERS) staff from 163 to 31; 86 House Democrats asked Secretary McMahon Monday to reverse the agreement, while House Republicans advanced a 10-bill package to make some transfers permanent. IDEA currently funds $15.5 billion in FY26 services for roughly 8.2 million students.

**So what:** Special ed is where education and health systems already overlap; how this transfer goes previews how an ED breakup would handle wellbeing-adjacent programs — and summit speakers named growing student mental health demand as a top unmet need.

## State Systems

### Mental health programs bear the brunt as $600M in federal funds is withheld from Texas schools
*The 74 · July 21, 2026* · [link](https://www.the74million.org/article/mental-health-programs-could-bear-the-brunt-of-600m-federal-cuts-to-texas-schools/)

At least $600 million — 16.1% of Texas's federal K-12 funding, per a Learning Policy Institute analysis of five frozen programs — is being withheld, and mental health programs are most exposed: two at-risk BSCA-funded programs (the Texas Center for Student Supports, in 60 high-need districts, and the Stronger Connections Grant Program, in 98 districts) account for 86% of school mental health funding across more than 2,500 campuses, per Mental Health America of Greater Houston. The state's new $8.5 billion school funding package is mostly earmarked for teacher pay; a dedicated student mental health allotment (HB 1257) failed, and only 13% of schools use the existing school safety allotment for mental health.

**So what:** The clearest downstream picture yet of what BSCA termination means: no state backfill exists, and the current allotment structure routes dollars to security over counselors.
