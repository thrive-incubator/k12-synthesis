# Thrive K12 Funding & Wellbeing Synthesis
### Week of 2026-06-24 – 2026-07-01

## This Week's Throughline

The week's sharpest wellbeing signal is a split screen on school mental-health money: Maryland committed **$96M** in new state funding for school mental-health services while, at the federal end, roughly **6,000 Maine students** stand to lose services because federal dollars are frozen in litigation. That is the state-vs-federal reliability gap in one frame — states are becoming the more dependable payer for school wellbeing as federal flows get tied up in courts and conditionality (see also the Trump administration's 10-day funding ultimatum to Jeffco). Downstream, districts are already spending state dollars on wellbeing (Elyria's **$5.5M**) and stitching wraparound supports (Detroit's Health Hubs), even as enrollment decline threatens the whole revenue base — an analysis pegs the hit at **$11.5B/year by 2030-31**, and districts from Alaska to Detroit are closing schools. On the vendor side, the big move is consolidation, not wellbeing: **ETS acquired ACT**, merging two assessment giants. The richest near-term opening for wellbeing ideas is non-dilutive: SAMHSA's **Trauma-Informed Services in Schools** grant is open and closes July 16, alongside HRSA's pediatric mental-health access program (closes July 10). Federal Register surfaced no K12 mental-health actions this week.
