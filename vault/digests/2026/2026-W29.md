# Thrive K12 Funding & Wellbeing Synthesis
### Week of 2026-07-08 – 2026-07-15

## This Week's Throughline

The spine of the week is a direct fight over the top of the school mental-health funding funnel: **15 state attorneys general sued the U.S. Department of Education to block the termination of roughly $1 billion in school mental-health grants**, with terminations taking effect in July and states alleging the administration is trying to circumvent an earlier court order protecting the money. Whatever the outcome, districts mid-grant now face months of staffing uncertainty in exactly the school-based mental-health workforce these programs were built to grow. Downstream, the austerity layer kept tightening — Chicago laid off 162 central-office staff, Rochester faces a $61M gap, and Detroit's operating revenue rides on an August 4 millage vote — which is the budget context any new wellbeing purchase has to survive. Meanwhile the classroom-AI land-grab met its first real procurement gate: Anthropic launched Claude for Teachers the same week NYC — the nation's largest district — held its pause on software purchases until AI guidance is final, a reminder that guidance documents are becoming the gatekeepers for all edtech, wellbeing tools included. In the ideas layer, litigation is doing as much work as appropriations: Boston sued Meta, TikTok, Snapchat, and YouTube over youth mental health, extending the legal-pressure channel as a shaper (and potential funder, via settlements) of the youth-wellbeing agenda. Near-term money: SAMHSA's **Trauma-Informed Services in Schools** NOFO closes **July 16** — tomorrow.
